Overview

This game is a realistic two-dimensional mass-spring network, which simulates physical bodies (such as ships) floating in water. You can punch holes in an object, slice it, apply forces to it, and smash it with bomb explosions. Once water starts rushing inside the object, it will start sinking and, if you're lucky enough, it will start breaking up!

Our philosophy in making this game is to simulate the real world as closely as possible. Each new release adds new ways in which particles interact among themselves and with the world that surrounds them; as of this release, Floating Sandbox contains the following simulations:

  • Gravity
  • Buoyancy - both underwater and in the atmosphere
  • Static pressure - hydrostatic pressure and atmospheric pressure
  • Drag - both underwater and in the atmosphere
  • Thermodynamics - with melting and combustion
  • Ocean floor impacts
  • Wind and waves
Tools

Once you load a ship, you can do quite a few things with it.
First of all, you can use the Smash tool to punch holes in it. The longer you keep the mouse button pressed, the larger the hole you can punch! Then there's the Slice tool, with which you can cut your ship into slices; hold the key to lock the direction of the cut.
Use the Blast tool instead to generate an explosive force field; the key shortens the blast dynamics for a more focused effect.
The Electric Spark tool sends jolts throughout the ship, causing the damage that jolts normally do - heat, burning, and interfering with electronics. Hold the key to boost the electric arcs' lengths.
The Attract tool and the Swirl tool apply radial and tangent forces, respectively. Also with these tools, the longer you keep the mouse button pressed, the stronger the forces become. You can invert the direction of the forces with the key.
The Pin tool allows you to "freeze" a point of the ship: once the point is frozen, its velocity becomes zero and its position never changes. It's best to increase the overall strength of the ship via the Simulator Settings window when you use this tool, or else the frozen point will quickly break under the enormous stress brought by neighboring points. An individual pin may be removed by applying the tool to the same point again, and all pins may be removed by pressing the key while applying the tool.
With the Move tool you can move (or rotate, if pressing the key) parts of a ship, while the Move All tool allows you to move (or rotate) an entire ship.
With the Pick'n'Pull tool you may pick a particle and move it around - but be careful, if you pull too quick it'll break!
The Inject Pressure tool allows you to inject pressure into a ship, or to remove pressure from it when pressing the key; similarly, the Flood tool allows you to inject water into a ship, or to extract water from it when pressing the key.
The HeatBlaster tool may be used to add heat to parts of the ship, sending them dangerously close to their burning point; pressing the key makes the tool remove heat instead.
After a ship has sunk to the bottom or has burnt for some time, its structure gets rusted and charred; you can use the Scrub tool to clean the dirt away. Pressing the key will turn the Scrub tool into a Rot tool, instead.
The WaveMaker tool may be used to manually generate waves, while the Adjust Terrain tool allows you to customize the ocean floor, creating abysses and islands as you see fit. Press the key to lock the terrain change horizontally.
The Scare tool can be used to...scare fishes away. Pressing the key turns the tool into yummy fish food, causing fishes to flock to your mouse cursor.

Finally, for the passive-aggressive types, ultimate destruction can be achieved lazily with bombs.
Timer Bombs explode automatically a few seconds after having been placed, while Remote Controlled Bombs and Anti-Matter Bombs await for you to detonate them with the and keys, respectively. Impact Bombs just explode on...impact.

If you feel remorse for the destruction you have inflicted, you may try the Repair Structure tool on the broken edges of a ship, and if you are skillful enough, you'll be able to restore the structure of the ship to its pristine condition.

NPC's - both human and furniture - may be added via the NPCs menu.

View Control

You can zoom the view with the and keys or with your mouse's wheel, while you can pan the view using the keys. If you get lost, the key resets the view to its default coordinates.
Darkness and light can be adjusted with the and keys.
